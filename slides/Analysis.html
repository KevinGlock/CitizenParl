Analysis

The corpus lingustical analysis on citizenship discourse is parted in three steps. The first step includes the macro effects of immigration rate. By analysing the correlation between immigration rate and term frequencies on Bundesebene. Secondly, contextualisation and argumentation on citizenship will be analysed on L?nderebene by using cooccurrence analysis. Further a classification scheme will be outworked.

long-term analysis

  • Salience of immigration

long-term analysis

Given keywords from the dictionary will be used for analysing the salience effect on speech frequencies regarding to citizenship. In comparing the immigration rate statistics with the frequency of tokens, correlation can be tested.

The immigration rate statistics come from the Federal Statitical Office of Germany (Statistisches Bundesamt).

dis <- dispersion("GERMAPARL",
                query = '"[Ss]taatsbrger.*"',
                cqp = TRUE,
                s_attribute = c("year",
                  "party")
                )

periodical salience

ts <- xts(x = dis[,
                  c(
  "CDU", "SPD", "GRUENE",
  "FDP", "LINKE")
  ],
  order.by = as.Date(
    sprintf(
      "%s-01-01",
      dis[["year"]])
    )
  )
